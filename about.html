About this site

KC Felder, BSN, RN

Registered Nurse · Clinical Informaticist · Cybersecurity Graduate Student

I spent 21 years in high-acuity clinical environments — ER, PACU, Surgical Services — where the cost of a bad decision is measured in patient outcomes, not quarterly reports. That background shapes everything about how I approach security.

I transitioned into clinical informatics as an Informatics Analyst III and Epic Instructional Designer, bridging the gap between complex technical systems and the clinicians who depend on them at 2 AM when something breaks. I learned what it means when a system fails in the real world — not in a post-mortem report, but in real time, with a patient on the table.

Now I'm completing my Master of Science in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance (MSCSIA) at Western Governors University, pursuing a focus on Governance, Risk, and Compliance. I hold the ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) credential and am working toward the CGRC and CRISC certification roadmap.

Why I Write

Because the LinkedIn post I read was right: certifications are the ticket to get in the door, but proof of work is what gets you the job. This blog is my proof of work — honest, in progress, showing the real thought process of someone learning this field from the inside out.

I am not a developer. I am not a penetration tester. I am someone who understands what is at risk when healthcare data is compromised, because I have held the hand of the patient that data belongs to.
