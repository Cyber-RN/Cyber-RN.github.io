About this site

KC Felder, BSN, RN

Registered Nurse · Clinical Informaticist · Cybersecurity Graduate Student

I spent 21 years in high-acuity clinical environments such as ER, PACU, and Surgical Services where the cost of a bad decision is measured in patient outcomes, not quarterly reports. That background shapes everything about how I approach security.

I transitioned into clinical informatics as an ER nurse to learn how to bridge the gap between complex technical systems and the clinicians who depend on them. I learned what it means when a system fails in the real world when there's an unscheduled downtime in the middle of an emergency surgery, not in a post-mortem report, but in real time, with a patient on the table.

Now I'm completing my Master of Science in Cybersecurity and Information Assurance (MSCSIA) at Western Governors University, pursuing a focus on Governance, Risk, and Compliance. I hold the ISC2 Certified in Cybersecurity (CC) credential and am working toward the CGRC and CRISC certification roadmap.

Why I Write

Because I've heard it said by many in the cybersecurity space, anyone can pass a test and get a cert. Certifications may get your foot in the door, but proof of work is what demonstrates the drive. This blog is my proof of work. It's honest and in progress, showing the real thought process of someone learning this field from the inside out.

I am not a developer. I am not a penetration tester. I am someone who understands what is at risk when healthcare data is compromised, because I have held the hand of the patient that data belongs to.
